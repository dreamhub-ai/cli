# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of the Dreamhub CLI project pledge to make
participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age,
body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity
and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that reflect Dreamhub's core values — **Quality**,
**Urgency**, and **Kindness** — and to contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive,
and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

* Demonstrating empathy, respect, and kindness toward other people
* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
* Taking responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning
  from the experience
* Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community
* Being transparent about AI tool usage in contributions (see
  [AI-Assisted Contributions](#ai-assisted-contributions) below)
* Considering the ethical implications of code, features, and integrations we propose
* Remembering that this is a global community — being patient with those for whom English
  is not a primary language

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without
  their explicit permission
* Using the Dreamhub CLI or its integrations to generate, distribute, or amplify content
  that is discriminatory, deceptive, or intended to cause harm
* Submitting AI-generated code or content without review, understanding, or appropriate
  disclosure
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Responsible AI Use

Dreamhub builds AI-native tools, and we hold our community to a high standard of responsible
AI practice. All participants in this project are expected to:

1. **Use AI capabilities ethically.** Do not use the Dreamhub CLI to produce outputs that
   promote discrimination, harassment, misinformation, or any form of harm against
   individuals or groups.
2. **Validate AI-generated outputs.** AI systems can produce inaccurate, biased, or
   misleading results. Always review, verify, and take responsibility for any AI-generated
   content before sharing it or acting on it.
3. **Respect data privacy.** Do not use the CLI to process, extract, or expose personal or
   sensitive data in ways that violate applicable privacy laws or the
   [Dreamhub Privacy Policy](https://www.dreamhub.ai/privacy-policy).
4. **Report harmful outputs.** If you discover that the CLI produces biased, discriminatory,
   or otherwise harmful outputs, please report the issue through our standard issue tracker
   or directly to the maintainers.

We believe that building trustworthy AI requires active participation from every contributor.
Ethical considerations are not a separate concern — they are central to the quality of our
work.

## AI-Assisted Contributions

We welcome the use of AI coding assistants (such as Copilot, Cursor, Claude, or similar
tools) to help with contributions. However, transparency and accountability remain with the
human contributor:

* **Disclose meaningful AI assistance.** If AI tools generated substantial portions of your
  code, documentation, or design, note this in your pull request description. Trivial
  autocomplete suggestions do not require disclosure.
* **Understand what you submit.** You must be able to explain, debug, and defend any code
  you contribute, regardless of how it was produced. Treat AI-generated code as untrusted
  input that requires your review.
* **Respect intellectual property.** Be mindful that AI-generated code may inadvertently
  reproduce licensed material. Ensure your contributions comply with the project's license.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders and project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our
standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in
response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments,
commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this
Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — including the GitHub repository,
issue tracker, pull requests, discussions, and any associated communication channels — and
also applies when an individual is officially representing the project or its community in
public spaces.

Examples of representing our project include using an official project email address, posting
via an official social media account, acting as an appointed representative at an online or
offline event, or engaging in community discussions on behalf of Dreamhub.

## Reporting and Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the
project maintainers at:

**[conduct@dreamhub.ai](mailto:conduct@dreamhub.ai)**

If your report concerns a maintainer or you are uncomfortable using the primary channel, you
may contact the Dreamhub team directly at **[info@dreamhub.ai](mailto:info@dreamhub.ai)**.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All community leaders
are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident. You can
expect an initial acknowledgment within **48 hours** of your report.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the
consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional
or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around
the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A
public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the
people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct,
for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as
well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or
permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained
inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with
the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the
people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct,
is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or
disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the project
community.
